PeaceSound

A low-arousal, evidence-informed sound generator for calming, sleep, meditation, and stillness. It combines brown-noise masking, gentle rain texture, and a very low non-melodic drone with long fades and no sudden events.

Safe-use target: keep the sound barely present. For overnight use, set your device so the audio is quieter than normal conversation and comfortable enough that you could sleep through it. This app cannot measure dBA.

Design logic

  • Brown noise: a low-frequency-weighted masking layer, less hissy than white noise.
  • Soft rain: an organic, predictable texture. No thunder, birds, bells, insects, voices, or sudden transients.
  • Low drone: a stable attentional anchor, kept very quiet for sleep and more present for meditation.
  • No miracle frequency claims: pitch is used for comfort, not “DNA repair,” awakening guarantees, or entrainment claims.
  • Autistic-first option: the sensory-simple preset starts with one continuous sound only, because layered sound can become overstimulating.
Technical notes

This uses the Web Audio API and an AudioWorklet for real-time synthesis. The brown layer is generated by leaky Brownian integration and then gently constrained. Rain is filtered, smoothed noise. The drone uses low sine partials with very slow amplitude drift, not binaural beats. A soft limiter prevents accidental digital clipping.

Recommended protocol

First use: start with Sensory-simple for 5 minutes. Add rain only if it feels calming. Add drone last. For sleep, reduce master volume until the sound is almost forgettable.

Not medical treatment. Stop if the sound causes distress, headache, ear pain, agitation, or worsened sensitivity. In a mental-health emergency, contact local emergency services or a crisis line.
